University of Wisconsin-Madison

Starting from 2020, I've been a student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. I'm planning graduating with a Comprehensive Honors through the College of Letters & Science in Computer Science, with a Mathematics minor. Outside of schoolwork, here's how I've been spending my time:

We strive for traditional dance, while at the same time, incorporating modern gimmicks and Western influences, especially hip hop. Ever since 2006, we have become one of the premier dance groups on campus and in Wisconsin. We have performed at many cultural shows and in competitions both on and off campus. In the future, we hope to not only spread the love of Bhangra in Wisconsin but also to the rest of the world through performing and competing.

Joining the Bhangra team was one of the best decisions I made in college. With them, I found the family environment I was seeking from my collegiate clubs. The shared passion for competition and dance turned WSB into something more than just a club.


Madtown Bhangra aims to celebrate South Asian culture in the U.S. by providing a platform for the nation's most talented collegiate Bhangra teams to perform. The organization strives to channel the competitive spirit of MTB towards fundraising for the various charity organizations chosen by the competing teams. Located in the vibrant Midwest campus town of Madison, MTB is a new central point for Bhangra teams from the East and West coast, as well as everywhere in between, to come together and showcase the rich Punjabi heritage and history through traditional music and dance. MTB's mission is to create a platform where all teams can showcase their love for Bhangra and support a charity that means the most to them.

After finding a great community with WSB, I decided to further my passion for Bhangra by helping organize the regional Bhangra competition. Just like I did with the badminton club, I leveraged my background coding websites to create a website for the competition.

Check out the website I made for Madtown Bhangra: here!


UW Badminton Club provides organized badminton play, camaraderie, and tournaments for UW-Madison students, faculty and staff.

Badminton has slowly been turning into one of my favorite sports. Through the club I played in a variety of different tournaments, even placing at the Steve Radar Memorial Open in 2022 and the Pioneer Open in 2023. I used to be a part of the administrative board at the UW Madison's Badminton Club, managing their social media and creating a website for the team.

Check out the website I made for the badminton club here!


We are dedicated to promoting a safe and inclusive community for Asian American students on the campus of UW-Madison.

A social club for Asian Americans. I joined with Henry and had a lot of fun the first year I was in it. The next year I joined as a Family Leader, which was a learning experience. I plan to continue commitment to the club as a family member.


Transcend is a student organization focused on providing invaluable resources and opportunities to students on campus who are interested in creating their own ventures. We do not limit our member’s ideas to tech, mobile applications or purely entrepreneurial ventures; instead we encourage collaboration between majors and focuses which we hope inspires start up ideas that can change the world.

I participated in Transcend to dip my toe into the world of entrepreneurship. Alongside Manisha and Harsh, I presented a new Flashcard alternative to Quizlet.


Wisconsin Surma is a co-ed Bollywood Fusion dance team at UW Madison. We compete nationally in a series of competitions alongside some of the best schools and teams in the country.

I wanted to find new ways to express my passion for music, so I tried out for Surma to learn how to dance, and made it. Unfortunately the entire season was during the COVID Pandemic, which made it a very limited experience.


We are a secular student organization at UW-Madison welcoming students of all backgrounds to come celebrate and learn about the Indian culture!

First Year Committee: I'd never tried any planning committee-esk organization before, so I joined the First Year Committee in the ISA to get an insight into what a planning committee does.

After that, I became a regular member of the club.


Coding For Good is a UW-Madison student organization that focuses on creating programs and applications that serve the community.

Coding for good is a hub for computer science projects. Each team works independently. The team I joined helped the Dane County Timebank transition their webpage to an updated version of Drupal.


High School

Although there were many activities that I participated in through High School, here is a brief snapshot of the more memorable ones.


I spent three years involved in Business Professional of America, and became a club captain in my second year.

BPA was a series of tournaments, at the regional, state, and national levels. In order to compete at the next level, you had to place high enough in the prior one. I made it to nationals two of the three years in which I was involved.


Soccer used to be my main sport, and I played for about ten years. I started out at the worst division of competitive play, and slowly made my way to the premier division of competitive soccer. When college came soccer departed from my list of priorities, however it will always have a spot in my heart.


I spent seven years playing the viola in an orchestra. Although we primarily played in school settings, we occasionally travelled, playing in Chicago and various churches across the midwest. When high school ended I no longer had an orchestra to play music with, so I discontinued playing the viola. However, the feeling of making music had already taken its hold on me. I turned to the guitar as a new sustainable outlet for my musicality, and started a Youtube channel on which I post guitar videos till this day.


Hindu Mandir Summer Camp was an amazing opportunity for both younger and older kids to learn more about their cultural background by participating in a week long camp, every eyar. Unfortunately I was only involved at the HMSC for two years, but it left me with memories I'll never forget.
