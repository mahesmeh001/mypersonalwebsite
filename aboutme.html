University of Wisconsin-Madison

Starting from 2020, I've been a student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. I'm planning graduating with a Comprehensive Honors through the College of Letters & Science in Computer Science, with a Mathematics minor. Outside of schoolwork, here's how I've been spending my time:

The Bhangra circuit was something I got involved in during my freshman year of college. This type of dance is from Punjab, and showcases the culture and history of this region. I was a part of the Madtown Bhangra Team from 2020-2022, and participated in events and competitions such as India Night 2022, and Buckeye Mela in Ohio in 2022.

I was a student at the Wisconsin School of Bhangra from 2020-2022. This school has kids ranging from age 5 to 20. At the time, I learned the fundamentals of Bhangra and its cultural context, as well as how to choreograph a dance routine.

During my second year of college, I was a member of the Badminton Club at UW-Madison. I regularly attended practices and games, and got to know many people who shared the same passion for badminton as me. It was a great way to stay active, de-stress, and have some fun!

I became involved with the Asian American Student Union in 2021. This organization has provided me with opportunities to learn about and celebrate my cultural heritage, connect with other students from similar backgrounds, and contribute to the community through various events and initiatives.

In my junior year, I joined Transcend, an organization focused on entrepreneurship and innovation. Through this group, I have had the chance to work on exciting projects, attend workshops and seminars, and network with professionals and fellow students who share an interest in startups and business development.

I joined Wisconsin Surma, a co-ed Bollywood Fusion Dance team, in my sophomore year. Through this team, I have participated in numerous performances and competitions, developed my dance skills, and made lasting friendships with fellow team members who share a passion for dance and culture.

As a member of the Indian Student Association, I have had the opportunity to engage with the Indian community at UW-Madison, celebrate Indian festivals and cultural events, and promote awareness and understanding of Indian culture among the wider university community.

I joined Coding For Good in 2021, a nonprofit organization that leverages technology to create positive social impact. Through this group, I have worked on projects that aim to address social issues, collaborated with other students and professionals, and developed my technical skills while contributing to meaningful causes.

High School

During my high school years, I was actively involved in various extracurricular activities that helped shape my interests and skills:

I was a member of the Business Professionals of America (BPA) during high school. BPA helped me develop my leadership and business skills, and provided opportunities to participate in competitions and network with other students who were interested in business and entrepreneurship.

I played soccer throughout high school, both for my school team and in local leagues. Soccer was a great way for me to stay active, build teamwork skills, and develop discipline.

I was a member of the school orchestra, where I played the violin. Being part of the orchestra helped me develop my musical skills, appreciate classical music, and work collaboratively with other musicians.

I attended the Hindu Mandir Summer Camp every year during high school. This camp was a wonderful opportunity for me to learn about my cultural and religious heritage, meet other students who shared similar backgrounds, and participate in various activities and workshops.

I was an active member of the Coding Club, where I developed my programming skills and worked on projects with other students who were passionate about coding and technology. This club sparked my interest in computer science and set the foundation for my future academic pursuits.
